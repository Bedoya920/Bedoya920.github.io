Bubble Typing

Global Game Jam 2025 | Core Gameplay & UI Systems

Project Overview

Bubble Typing is an ergonomics-focused typing game engineered to promote digital health while retaining engaging core loops. Developed entirely under the strict 48-hour timeline of the Global Game Jam 2025 under the theme "Bubbles", it balances functional benefits with immediate entertainment. By spending just a few minutes a day, users can actively reduce the risk of repetitive strain injuries (RSI) such as tendonitis, carpal tunnel syndrome, and standard postural fatigue.

My Contribution & Engineering Role

Within this multidisciplinary sprint, my responsibility centered on architecting the core UI/UX execution frameworks, dynamic scene states, spawner logic matrices, and procedural system navigation. I implemented clean transition structures between runtime modules, making sure menu elements handled game loops without leaks.

While our technical artists built the custom vector assets, I bridged their production with code-driven reactive physics, allowing developers to safely implement decoupled score logic, persistent data tracking setups, and optimal collision routines concurrently.
